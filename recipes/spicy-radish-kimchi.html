Spicy Radish Kimchi (Kkakdugi)

Description

Kkakdugi is a very common kimchi and used often used in Korean daily meals. This radish kimchi brings a nice spice, and numerous health benefits including probiotic properties.

  • Korean Radish(or Daikon)
  • Kosher Salt
  • Sugar
  • Korean Hot Pepper Flakes
  • Soy Sauce
  • Green Onions
  • Minced Garlic
  • Minced Ginger
  1. Peel / dry radish, cut into cubes, and place into a bowl.
  2. Add salt and sugar, then set aside for 30 minutes.
  3. Drain the juice from the radish into a small bowl.
  4. Add garlic, ginger, green onions, pepper flakes, and 1/3rd of a cup of juice from the radish.
  5. Mix well until the seasonings coat the radish cubes evenly.
  6. Put the mix into a glass jar and press down on the top of it to remove any air from the radish cubes.
  7. You can enjoy right away, or let the mix ferment by keeping it outside of the refrigerator. When it starts fermenting, it will begin to smell sour. Then put it in the refrigerator.
